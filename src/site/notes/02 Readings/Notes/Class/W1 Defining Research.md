#### Defining the object of study
*Write a (max. 150-word) description of what you intend to do for your Capstone Project and bring it to class. Your project idea should be a natural extension of your work to date, drawing on your existing expertise but doing something new with it.*

Heres my Draft. I still keep flipflopping a bit over the framework vs the creative output. And i havent really worked out my research question/s  
  
I intend to investigate how speculative worlds can be designed to be experienced across interconnected media forms, rather than told through a single linear story. Building on my work in digital production, UX, roleplaying, interactive design, creative technology, and game systems, I will develop a practical “worldtelling” (TBC) framework for players/users, authors and designers who want audiences to explore a world through different levels of transmedia participation.  
  
The project will use Immutable Aeon, my own "post-cyberpunk" TTRPG setting, as both creative material and a test case. While the world itself will continue to develop through the project, the main object of study is the design system: how story, setting, interaction, platform, and player agency can work together to create meaning. The outcome will likely include a prototype or vertical slice, supported by an exegesis reflecting on the design process, relevant theory, and the framework’s usefulness for creative practice.

Love this quote

There is nothing like looking, if you want to find something. You certainly usually find something, if you look, but it is not always quite the something you were after. – J. R. R. Tolkien

- My experience can generate the research question, but it cannot be the whole argument. I need to reflect on it, question it, and support it through literature and practice. 
- Research is not just collecting ideas or making a thing. It needs a clear question, a process, and reflection on what the making reveals. 
	- Systematic - steps, procedures, and a process.
	- Organised - structure - it can change, but not spontenously, 
	- Central question - creates focus, drive, or purpose.
- Research is iterative Figure I.1
	- This reflects my own process. I keep opening the project up, then narrowing it again. That is not necessarily a problem, as long as I eventually focus the question and make the process visible.
	- The cycle gives me permission to revise the project as I learn, but not to stay vague forever.
	- (Opening up is where I explore ideas, read broadly, make strange, question assumptions, and resist quick decisions.)
- My capstone needs to show how the creative work answers a research question. The speculative world is the test site. The framework is the thing being developed through practice.
