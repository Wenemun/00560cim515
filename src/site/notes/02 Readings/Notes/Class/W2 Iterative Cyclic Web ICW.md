[CANDY-PracticeBasedResearchCreative-2018.pdf](/img/user/02%20Readings/PDFs/CANDY-PracticeBasedResearchCreative-2018.pdf)  

- Research is making a thing to answer a question and contribute to shared knowledge, not just my knowledge.
- The question often evolves as you make the thing. The making itself is part of the investigation.
- The world/game/artifact isn't the contribution by itself. What it teaches us is.
- Practice and research are not the same thing.
- "I learned something" isn't enough. It needs to be useful beyond just myself.
- The artefact shows the work. The exegesis explains why it matters.

This view could somewhat exclude plans of being able to commercialise my work/findings.  
  
Definitions:  
Practice-based = the thing you make is part of the contribution.  
Practice-led = the contribution is more about principles, methods, frameworks, or understanding.  
Take away:  
My game world, Immutable Aeon might be the vehicle, but the actual research contribution is going to be the framework/system/principles/insights etc that come out of building it as "transmedia first", or "setting first" (not world first, to stop confusion)

ICW -  
initial reaction: feels pretty familiar. It's basically how I create.  
Ideas lead to making. Making leads to questions. Questions lead to research. Research leads back to making.  
It acknowledges the messiness of creative practice. I don't have to start with a fixed question and follow a straight line.  
The flip side is that it feels like a formal academic justification for scope creep. Every path leads to three more interesting paths.  
The biggest takeaway for me is that the question can evolve through making, and that's okay. Immutable Aeon isn't really the destination, it's the sandbox. The interesting part is what building it teaches me along the way.
