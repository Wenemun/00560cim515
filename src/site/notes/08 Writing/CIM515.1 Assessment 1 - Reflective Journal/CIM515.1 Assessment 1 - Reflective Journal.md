**Brief**

A reflective journal allows you to: 

- Synthesise new concepts in ways that are meaningful to you 
    
- Practice writing for a general academic audience using clear and precise language
    
- Share your ideas with your community of practice and receive feedback and guidance on your ideas as they progress
    

**Requirements**

You are required to:

- Publicly share and organise your journal entries so that your peers and mentors/supervisors can access and comment on it
    
- Respond directly to the journal prompts in the weekly content
    
- Employ referencing in APA style for ideas that are not your own
    
- Focus on your own synthesis, application, and creative ideation based on the key concepts, rather than simply restating the unit themes
    
- Include an expedited ==ethical checklist==, and highlight and discuss any need for further ethical clearance https://docs.google.com/document/d/13TFzavASxrIKJz0DTqmlzNpz--fhSh29CDVleVQzuHk/edit?tab=t.0
    
- Allow for the development of your research topic in response to the literature, feedback, and other themes you encounter during the unit.
    

In addition, you are ==required to attend Graduate Seminar== so that you hear the different ways that practitioners communicate  their creative research. For this assessment you are required to: 

- Write short reflections on two (2) Graduate Seminar presentations
