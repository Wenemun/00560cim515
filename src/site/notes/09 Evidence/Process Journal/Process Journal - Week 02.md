That distinction can be summed up as follows :
• If a creative **artifact is the basis** of the contribution to knowledge, the research is **practice-based.**
insights from making, reflecting and evaluating may be fed back directly into the artifact itself. 

• If the research leads primarily to **new understandings about practice**, it is **practice-led.** The outcomes may be shared in the form of principles, models, frameworks and guidelines.

## Version 1: Framework First

This version focuses on creating a practical ==framework== for worldtelling across multiple media forms.

The research area is how a speculative world can be designed so people can experience it in different ways: watching, reading, exploring, choosing, or playing.

The output would be a framework, manifesto, or design principles/toolkit, supported by diagrams and examples from Immutable Aeon. The research insight would come from making the framework, testing it against my own world, and reflecting on what changes through the process.

This is achievable alone because it does not necessarily need collaborators, testers, or human participants. The evidence would be process notes, diagrams, drafts, literature notes, and reflective analysis.

The insight comes from designing the system and reflecting on what principles emerge.

## Version 2: Prototype First

This version focuses on making a small ==interactive prototype==.

The research area is how a speculative world can be experienced through an interactive system that blends story, choice, exploration, and media.

The output could be a short visual novel, interactive website, [Twine](https://twinery.org/)- style piece, and/or Unreal vertical slice. It would use one small part of Immutable Aeon rather than trying to build the whole world.

This version is more creative and tangible, but also riskier because it could grow too large. To keep it achievable, I would limit it to one short arc with no external testers or participants. The evidence would be screenshots, builds, design notes, version history, and reflection.

The insight comes from testing how the world feels when someone can move through it, choose, or interact.

## Version 3: World Bible as Research Artefact

This version focuses on the ==world of Immutable Aeon== as the main research artefact.

The research area is how a speculative world can be structured to support multiple stories, platforms, player roles, and levels of agency.

The output would be a world bible, but designed as an experience system, not just lore. It could include factions, timelines, locations, characters, terminology, media pathways, and examples of how one world element changes across different formats.

This is achievable alone because it is based on writing, mapping, design, and reflection. The main risk is becoming too lore-heavy, so I would need to keep linking each decision back to multimodal experience design.

These additional concepts could also be included in either of the above 3.

#### **4. Make a media map**  
Explore the question by mapping how one world element (character place faction event object) could appear across different forms: website, game, tabletop, video, fake archive, audio log, faction document, etc. The insight comes from seeing how the same world changes depending on the medium. - ie how the experiential qualities - meaning, function, form and relationship are reshaped when designed across different media.

This is similar to something I did for HSC, so i don't think its suitable for masters. Interesting under a new lens however.

#### **5. Create a narrative web**  
Explore the question by designing a branching story structure (ie using a simple VN) with multiple paths, choices and consequences. The practice-based insight comes from analysing the **range of agency** the system can support: from small interpretive choices, to meaningful narrative decisions, to larger actions that reshape characters, factions, locations or world states. This allows the project to examine not only the minimum agency needed for a storyworld to feel inhabitable, but also the maximum extent of agency possible before the experience becomes incoherent, unmanageable or detached from the intended themes.

This would be less sandbox or open world though, and only be applicable to determining the extents of the platform i build it on.

Something like "What is the viable range of agency in a world-led storytelling system, and how can that agency be expanded without breaking coherence?"

| Level of agency        | What changes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Interpretive agency    | The user interprets meaning, tone, clues or lore in the narrative, to choose different dialogues.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Navigational agency    | The user chooses order, path or point of entry to the adventure. For this I'd implement levels nodally and interlink the nodes with dialogue choices from the other nodes.                                                                                                                                                         |
| Tactical agency        | The user makes local choices within a scene or encounter - limit the choices so it only affects the specific scene, not the rest of the world.                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Narrative agency       | The user changes story outcomes or inter-character relationships.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| World-state agency     | The user affects factions, locations, histories or future conditions - this makes their missions mean something - the way i would incorporate this is at the chapter or arc level.                                                                                                                                                 |
| Strategic world agency | The user can meaningfully alter the (world) rules, structures or direction of the world - User creates factions and NPCs or changes political and resource systems, social rules, or laws. This would mean a very different game from what I imagined, more like Civilization, Kingmaker, Kenshi or a Wargame Campaign than a RPG. |
**minimum agency** = enough to move from passive consumption into inhabitation.  
**maximum agency** = the edge where freedom starts to destabilise the experience.
