## Practice
*What did I make, write, test, read, revise, or decide?*

Lots of thinking, significant reading.

Thought through Assessment 1 and 2, the pitch structure, and the literature areas.

I also worked out that the framework is not really the final artwork. The framework is more like the canvas or structure I am making the artwork on.

The artwork is the game / interactive / transmedia experience.

I will use this to answer the question/s that I've yet to actually clarify - but even then, these questions can change as the project progresses, through iteration.

***How can a worldtelling framework support the design of a speculative interactive game experience?***

(***How can worldtelling be used to design a speculative interactive game experience?***)

- World - How can a speculative world be revealed through play, interaction, interface, and narrative rather than explained through lore alone?
- Experience - How can different levels of audience agency shape the way players enter, explore, and understand the world?
- Framework - What design principles emerge when worldtelling is used as the canvas for creating the experience?
- Practice - How does my own creative practice change through making, reflecting, testing, and revising the work?

## Why
*Why did this matter to the capstone?*

This matters because the project was starting to feel too big and vague.

I need to be clearer before the pitch, otherwise it sounds like I am trying to build a full game, full world, full transmedia platform, and full framework all at once.

## Self
*What did this reveal about my habits, assumptions, taste, background, scope creep, or way of working?*

This showed my usual habit of wanting to build the whole system.

I keep expanding the idea because I can see how all the parts connect, but that does not mean they all need to be in the capstone.

I need to keep reminding myself: vision, not full version.

## Practice
*What changed in the framework, prototype, artefact, world, writing, or design process?*

The project shifted from “I am making a framework” to “I am making a game/experience using a framework as the canvas.”

That feels more accurate.

The framework still matters, but it supports the creative work rather than replacing it.

## Shift
*What changed in my thinking or project direction?*

The big shift is that the creative artefact is now clearer.

It is not just a framework.

It is a speculative interactive experience, supported by a worldtelling framework.

That makes the project feel easier to explain.
